The **Documents** menu on a division or competition generates the paperwork you hand out or print for matchday: a timetable for the venue, blank scoresheets for the table, a rota for your officials, and tickets for the gate. Each is a real export you download — nothing is emailed automatically.

## Order of play

The timetable: every fixture's time, court and entrants for the day, in schedule order. Useful pinned to a noticeboard or handed to a venue manager who has no console access.

## Match sheets

Blank scoresheets, one per fixture, ready to print and clip to a board — for scorers working on paper instead of the live console, or as a backup if a device dies mid-match.

## Officials rota

Every assignment for the day, grouped by official: who's covering which fixture, court and time. The same data your officials see on their own `/me` home, laid out for a printed sheet at the officials' table.

## Admit tickets

One ticket per entrant (or per seat, for team/pair events), each carrying a **QR code**. Scanning it at the gate opens the entrant's status page — the fastest way to confirm who's checked in without looking anyone up by name.

## PDF vs XLSX

Order of play and match sheets are tabular, so both export as **PDF** (for printing) or **XLSX** (for reworking in a spreadsheet — reordering courts, adding notes, importing into another system). Admit tickets are **PDF only** — they're designed to be printed or shown on a phone at the gate, not edited.

## Branded vs plain

On **Pro**, every export carries your organisation's masthead — logo, name and a tier-grouped sponsor line — so anything you hand out or print looks like it came from your event. On the free plan the same documents export as clean, unbranded tables: same data, same layout, no masthead or sponsor line.

## The personal rota on `/me`

An official doesn't need console access to get their own day's schedule: their `/me` home has a **Download my rota** button in the officiating lane, exporting just their own assignments as a PDF — no need to wait for someone to hand them the full officials rota.
