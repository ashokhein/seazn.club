Once a fixture is **decided**, you can mark the officials who covered it — a 1–5 score with an optional comment, the way FA clubs mark referees every weekend. Marking is a **Pro feature** (`officials.marks`); Community sees the upgrade prompt in its place.

## Rate an official

On a division's **schedule → Officials tab**, the **Rate officials** section lists every decided fixture that had an *accepted* official. Tap one of the five scoreboard tiles — **1 to 5** — to set the mark; the lit tile is your score. There are no stars: digits are the scoreboard vernacular. One tap saves it. You can change or clear a mark any time — leagues correct marks — and there is **one mark per assignment**.

Marking only opens for an **accepted** assignment on a **decided or finalized** fixture: you can't rate someone who declined, and you can't rate a match that hasn't been played.

## See an official's average

In **Directory → Officials**, choose **Marks** on any official to see their **average** as a scorebug badge, the **count**, and the **last five comments** with their fixture labels. This aggregate is **your organisation's marks only**.

## Privacy — marks are org-private

Marks never leave your console. The official sees **only their own running average across every league**, and only once at least **3 marks** exist — never an individual mark, never a comment, never which organisation gave what. Marks do **not** appear on public pages, shared dashboards or any cross-org directory, and there are no referee league tables.

## Marks bind to the performance, not the result

A mark rates how the official *performed*, so it stays even if the result is later voided — voiding a fixture's result never voids the performance the mark rated. The mark keeps counting in the average.
