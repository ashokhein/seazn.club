---
title: Your stats and photo
description: Claimed players see their season totals on their public profile and manage their own photo from Me.
order: 3
---

**Stats on your profile.** A public player profile now shows per-division totals — goals, assists, cards, awards, whatever the sport tracks — pulled straight from the scored matches. There's nothing to enter: play, get scored, the numbers appear. The stat labels come from the sport itself, so a cricketer sees runs and wickets where a footballer sees goals. Two separate things have to be true for a public profile to exist. **Your consent:** it appears only for a player who has turned *show my name publicly* on — no plan overrides that, and switching it off takes the card down again. **The club's plan:** public player cards need **Pro or Pro Plus**, or an [Event Pass](/help/billing/event-pass) on that competition; a Community club has no public cards for anyone. The leaderboard table in the organiser console remains a Pro feature.

**Stats on Me.** The same numbers live under **My stats** on your own **Me** page, on any plan — every competition you play in, private ones included, because they're yours. Where the competition is public, the club's plan carries player cards and your name consent is on, each block links to your public profile.

**Your photo, yours.** From **Me**, next to your privacy toggles, upload or remove your own photo — no need to ask an organiser, on any plan. It shows publicly only while your *show my photo* consent is on — a separate switch from your name — and only where the club's plan puts a public card up at all. Profiles of under-16 players stay organiser-managed.
