If you officiate a match, you can file a **match report** afterwards — what happened, plus structured incident rows for cards, misconduct, injuries and anything else the organiser should know. Filing a report is **free on every plan**: the officiating portal never asks you to upgrade.

## Where to file

Open your **`/me` home**, expand **Completed matches** in the officiating lane, and choose **File match report** on the match. The button shows the state: *File match report*, *Finish match report* (a saved draft), or *Match report filed*.

A report opens once the match has **ended** — decided, finalized or **abandoned** (abandonments are exactly when a report matters) — and only for an assignment you **accepted**.

## Write it

- **What happened** — a short summary. It saves as a **draft** automatically as you go.
- **Incidents** — add a row per incident: pick the **kind** (red card, misconduct, injury, other), optionally pick the **player** from either squad, and write a **note**.
- **Submit report** — you'll confirm once. **A submitted report is final** — you can't edit it afterwards; if something needs changing, ask the organiser.

## What happens next

Submitting notifies the organisation's admins and surfaces your report on their fixture console. If the league runs **discipline**, a **red-card or misconduct** incident that names a player is turned into a **suggested pending suspension** — the organiser reviews and confirms it; nothing is banned automatically. Reports are private to the organiser; they never appear on public pages.
