description: Nothing is deleted — anything over Community limits becomes read-only until you make room, in every organisation the subscription paid for.

Cancel Pro and you return to Community limits. The rule is simple: **nothing is deleted, ever.**

## It applies to every organisation on the subscription

Read this first if you pay for more than one. A subscription is a [billing group](/help/billing/groups), and the plan belongs to the group — so cancelling doesn't drop one organisation, it drops **all of them, at the same moment**. Eight clubs billed through one Pro Plus subscription become eight Community organisations the day the period ends. The same goes for a failed renewal that runs out of grace: the group degrades together, not one club at a time.

Nothing is deleted in any of them. Each organisation keeps everything it had and freezes what's over the limit, exactly as described below, and each keeps its own Stripe connection and its own money — see [payouts never move](/help/billing/groups).

If it's only one organisation you want off the plan, don't cancel: **remove it from the group** instead. That leaves the others paying and running as normal, and the one that leaves keeps its plan until the period you've already paid for runs out.

## A failed renewal has a grace period

If a renewal payment fails, nothing changes straight away. Stripe retries the card for **14 days** (this is called dunning) and the subscription stays on Pro the whole time — so a lapsed card or a bank blip never yanks features mid-tournament. Only if those 14 days pass with no successful payment do the organisations it pays for fall back to Community limits, and the moment a payment does go through, Pro is restored immediately, everywhere at once. Update your card under **Settings → Plan & Billing** to clear it sooner.

## The freeze

Limits are counted **per organisation**, so each one freezes against its own Community allowance rather than sharing a single pool. In each, anything over a Community limit becomes **read-only**: the most recently active competitions stay fully editable up to the limit; the rest freeze. A frozen competition's dashboards, standings and history all remain visible — you just can't change it until there's room.

## Making room

- **Archive** or complete competitions until you're inside the limits — frozen ones thaw automatically.
- Or upgrade again; everything unfreezes instantly.

Pro-only features (your brand colour on public pages, exports, API keys…) simply switch off rather than freezing anything — public pages fall back to the standard look, keys stop authenticating. Your **club logo stays** either way; it's on every plan.

## Card entry fees keep working — the cut goes back up

Worth stating plainly, because it used to work the other way: **registration doesn't stop taking money.** Card entry fees run on every plan, Community included, so divisions collecting by card carry on taking sign-ups, every organisation keeps its Stripe connection, and every existing paid entry and its money are untouched.

What changes is the platform fee. Community's cut is **8%**, against 2% on Pro and 1% on Pro Plus, and it applies to payments taken from the moment you drop back — earlier entrants keep the rate they paid at. A competition covered by an [Event Pass](/help/billing/event-pass) charges 5% instead. See [how card entry fees flow](/help/registration/card-payments).

## Common questions

**Do frozen results disappear from public pages?** No — read-only means the record stands; visitors notice nothing.

**Extra team members?** Seats over the Community limit become read-only members until the owner frees seats — counted per organisation, like every other limit.

**Can I keep some organisations on Pro and let one go?** Yes, and that's the right move: take that organisation out of the group rather than cancelling. Cancelling is all-or-nothing for everything the subscription pays for. See [one subscription, several organisations](/help/billing/groups).

**What if a member of one organisation has nothing to do with the others?** They won't notice the billing at all — membership and payment are separate. They only see their own organisation's limits change.
